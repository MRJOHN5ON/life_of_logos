Brand & logo

Cory needed a name and mark that felt like him, not a generic institute template. I designed the Classes with Cory wordmark and circular seal — navy, academic gold, and warm cream — used in the header, footer, favicon path, and across every page of the rebuild.

  • ✓ Distinct from the old Lingua Franca Weebly branding
  • ✓ Works at small sizes (nav mark) and large (hero trust)
  • ✓ Same system on site + deliverable logo files for print and social

Two languages · one site

Cory teaches in both languages — his site should too. Visitors tap English (🇺🇸) or Spanish (🇨🇴) in the header and the whole page updates: nav, services, about, contact. No second site to maintain, no Weebly language upsell.

Real portrait · AI scenes

Cory had one great portrait session — enough for the homepage, not enough for every class page, banner, and contact section. Instead of booking another shoot, I generated on-brand scene images from his likeness: classrooms, travel, coaching — so the site looks full and professional from day one.

  • → Homepage leads with his real photo — students see the actual teacher
  • → Supporting images match his brand without a second photoshoot bill
  • → When he has new photos, drop them in — same layout, no redesign

What 3 years on a builder costs

Roughly what you keep paying just to stay on someone else’s platform — public list prices (2025–2026). Cory was on Weebly (highlighted).

Platform Typical plan (approx.) ~3 years of subscriptions
Weebly · this client ~$10–$26/mo ~$360–$940+
Wix ~$17–$36/mo ~$610–$1,300+

Figures are ballpark only — annual billing and add-ons can push totals higher. This is subscription rent, not a website you own.

Pay once · own forever

You pay me once for the website build. You own the code after that. Ongoing cost is usually just your domain (~$20/year) and low-cost hosting — not Weebly every year.

Your site · your data

If Weebly or Squarespace goes down, changes terms, or you lose access, your content is hostage. A custom site means you own the files and your data — forever.

Take it anywhere

Platform-specific designers are a niche market. Coded websites are maintained everywhere. What I build is standard web tech — portable, not locked in.
