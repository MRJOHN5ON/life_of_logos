Free tools = free marketing

Four calculators and a plant ID tool — free, no login, no account. Homesteaders, firewood sellers, and landowners use them; Givens gets the call when they're ready to hire. Each one is its own Google landing page with a quote button at the bottom.

Firewood cord calculator

How much firewood is actually in that pile? Measures real tapered logs, not a fantasy 4×4×8 stack. Cord counts, stack diagrams, and plain-English guides — the kind of tool people bookmark and share. (Under the hood: frustum volume math, not a generic widget.)

Tree height calculator

Measure a tree from the ground — five different field methods, metric or imperial, print or save the result. Landowners figure out clearance and removal tier before Samuel rolls up. (Angle, shadow, clinometer, triangulation — full surveyor toolkit in one page.)

Wildfire risk calculator

Type an address, get a wildfire risk score for that property — fuel on the ground, slope, live weather, 16-day outlook. Built by a Montana contractor who actually does defensible-space work, not a generic insurance map. Leads straight into a consult. (Powered by LANDFIRE federal data + Rothermel fire-behavior modeling.)

Plant & tree identifier

Master identity · shield

Same shield · now 3D

From flat mark to hero crest

The shield started as a 2D logo for trucks and print. For the owned homepage I took that same artwork into Blender, extruded the metal face, built a cut-log wood backing that sits under the rim, and shipped it as a real GLB that rotates in the browser with model-viewer.

No stock badge pack. Same brand Samuel already has on the truck — just with depth, material, and a little presence when the page loads.

  • → Exact logo outline extruded in Blender
  • → Cast-iron rim + steel faces + end-grain wood core
  • → Wood fitted under the outer rim (no halo, no gaps)
  • → Fades in when ready — no flash of the old 2D poster
